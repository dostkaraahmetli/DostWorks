Foreword¶

With this report I will try to make explanatory data analysis on the European Soccer Database, as well as trying to answer two interesting questions and making inferences about them.

For this purpose, I decided to extract four datasets from the SQLite database;

  • Country
  • League
  • Match
  • Player Attributes (to be analysed seperately)

I will try to answer the following research questions:

  • Is there really a so called home advantage?
  • What are the most important skills (attributes) contributing to the overall rating of a player?

A. Dataset and Pre-processing¶

Dataset: European Soccer Database https://www.kaggle.com/hugomathien/soccer

Some facts about the dataset:

  • +25,000 matches
  • +10,000 players
  • 11 European Countries with their lead championship
  • Seasons 2008 to 2016
  • Players and Teams' attributes* sourced from EA Sports' FIFA video game series, including the weekly updates
  • Detailed match events (goal types, possession, corner, cross, fouls, cards etc...) for +10,000 matches

C. Inferential Statistics¶

Playing at home is almost certainly an advantage. Otherwise, the mean goal difference (calculated above) would be zero. However, it is 0.4 for our dataset.

I'd like to run a one sample t-test to compare the mean goal difference of our sample (0.4), to the expected mean goal difference without home advantage (0).

H0 -> Average goal difference between home and away goals does not differ from the expected average difference (0).

  • H0: µgd = 0 Ha -> Average goal difference between home and away goals is larger than 0.
  • Ha: µgd > 0

Reject H0 if p-value ≤ α P-value is so small that we don't even have to look at the t-Table. Thus, we reject H0.

Conclusion:¶

We have strong evidence that average difference between home and away goals differ from the expected average difference, in favor of home goals. (LoS = 0.05, p-value = 1.027e-159)

This difference is considered to be statistically significant, so we can say that there is indeed an advantage for teams playing in their home stadium.
